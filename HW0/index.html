Double Dream of Pearls

  • Originality: What is original about your game?
    • My game ups the challenge of the classic Breakout by making the player manage two "pearls."
    • I also wanted to create a summery theme with the beach and ocean-related sprites and music.
  • Technical Merit: What is interesting about your game technology?
    • The game engine has to account for two physical bodies--the pearls--instead of just one.
    • This didn't mean just doubling all the places there was a ball. Some interactions affect both, others just one.
  • Prototype Postmortem: What did you learn from this prototype? What was the easiest or hardest part of making it?
    • Making the music and finding the sprites was fun. I felt I had the most stylistic control in these parts.
    • Getting the two pearls to work was more difficult than I expected. There are still some issues--for example, they won't both spawn on the clam!

You may include mock-up images for parts of the game not in the prototype.
  • First, the player clicks or taps to release the pearls. Then, the player moves the clam from side to side using a mouse or by sliding on a touchscreen. The player wants to make sure the pearls bounce off the clam when they are headed towards the bottom of the screen--or else, they will lose a life! When a pearl collides with a block, it will break it, and open up new directions for the pearls to bounce in. A player can rack up a lot of points by getting a pearl to bounce between two rows of blocks, hitting them all. To win the game, a player must break all of the blocks without losing all of their pearls.
  • Strategies: What player strategies do you expect will be effective at playing this game?
    • Make sure to keep track of the location of both pearls at all times!
    • Bouncing the pearl between two rows will rack up lots of points.
    • Try to space out the pearls so that you won't have to bounce both back at the same time.
